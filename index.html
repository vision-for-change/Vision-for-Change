Our Mission

Founded by Youth,
For Everyone.

Vision for Change is run by passionate young people who believe clear sight shouldn't depend on your zip code or bank balance. We're taking the first steps to make that a reality.

Three Pillars

How We Pursue Our Mission

01
📚
Educate Communities
Spreading awareness about the importance of eye health, common vision problems, prevention strategies, and the power of early detection before conditions worsen.
02
🚧
Remove Barriers
Reducing obstacles by raising awareness about costs, connecting people with available resources, and advocating for policy changes that improve access to affordable eye care.
03
🌍
Inspire Action
Driving campaigns, community advocacy, and fundraising toward the ultimate goal: affordable eye exams and glasses available to every single person who needs them.
04
🤝
Build Community
Growing a network of youth volunteers, donors, and supporters who share our belief that vision care is a right — and are willing to act on that belief together.
Our Vision

A World Where Sight Isn't a Privilege

We envision a world where clear vision is a right, not a privilege — where your ability to see isn't determined by your income, your geography, or the circumstances of your birth.

We are taking the first steps to make that a reality in our communities, with the goal of expanding our reach year by year.

"Vision is a gift that 80% of the world's visually impaired people could receive with proper care. We refuse to accept that as the status quo."

— Vision for Change Team, 2026

Eye Health

Simple Habits.
Lasting Sight.

Your eyes work harder than any other organ. Four simple habits can protect your vision for a lifetime — here's what actually matters.

Protect Your Vision

Four Tips That Actually Work

WHO Resources
01
🖥️
Screen Breaks

The 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Reduces digital eye strain dramatically and prevents long-term damage from screens.

↗
02
🥕
Nutrition

Vitamins A, C, E, zinc, and omega-3s protect against macular degeneration and cataracts. Eat leafy greens, colourful vegetables, and oily fish regularly.

↗
03
👁️
Regular Checkups

Glaucoma and macular degeneration have no symptoms until significant damage occurs. Annual eye exams catch these conditions early when treatment is most effective.

↗
04
🕶️
Sun Protection

UV exposure accumulates over a lifetime. Sunglasses with 100% UVA/UVB protection prevent cataracts, macular degeneration, and photokeratitis.

↗
Trusted Source

Learn More from the World Health Organization

The WHO tracks global vision data and provides the most reliable, up-to-date information on eye health, blindness prevention, and access to care worldwide.
